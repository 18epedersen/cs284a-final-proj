Title, Summary and Team Members

Title

Battling Humans VR game

Summary

Taking inspiration from the popular Nintendo fighting game Super Smash Bros, we’d like to make a VR game for fighting between human-like avatars. We will model human skin, hair, specific types of clothing fabric, and hopefully how skin reacts during collisions (during the multi-playing fighting).

Problem Description

Here you should provide the context for your idea. Describe the problem that you are trying to solve, why it is important, where it is challenging. Give us a general idea on how you are going to solve it.

The purpose of our project is for entertainment. We plan to make a virtual reality fighting video game. An interesting component of video games is the immersion of the player, which can be greatly heightened through the use of VR. The capabilities of VR are constantly expanding and we thought it would be interesting to explore these by converting a well-known video game type to VR for an added sense of realism.

This will be especially challenging in the realm of graphics. Since we are trying to add elements of realism into the typical fighting video game, we will be especially focused on the materials of the fighting opponent and how these react to collisions. Materials associated with the human body (i.e. skin, hair, clothing etc…) are already deemed as difficult to realistically render, so adding the collision element to these materials will be even more challenging. Another challenge is making sure the render times of the video game are fast enough for VR. Since VR requires a player to place the screen over their eyes using goggles the game would need to render at 90 frames per second to avoid the player becoming dizzy or nauseous.

We plan to have a VR fighting video game in which the player will fight against another humanoid character. Our goal is to add extra realism into the typical console fighting game by using virtual reality to further immerse the player into the experience.

Goals and Deliverables

If you are working on an interactive system, describe what demo you will create.

Our goal for this project would be to have a virtual reality fighting game in which the player can fight against another player, or computer character. Our demo would allow the player to move around the VR map and fight their opponent using punches and kicks. The opponent would be another humanoid type character with skin, hair, and clothing. These materials would realistically react to the collisions from the hits of the player. In this demo each player would have a health bar which would decrease whenever being punched or kicked by the opponent. The game would end when one player loses all of their health.

Define how you will measure the quality/performance of your system (e.g. graphs showing speedup, or quantifying accuracy). It may not be possible to define precise target metrics at this time, but we encourage you to try.

We will measure the quality and performance of our system by tracking (1) the frames per second (fps) of our game, (2) how accurately the VR system can respond to human motion input, (3) how accurately collision is detected, especially with models with moving parts, (4) the response of meshes to collisions, (5) the smoothness of model animations, especially when transitioning between different animations, (6) how appropriate the lighting is, and (7) how balanced the gameplay is.
